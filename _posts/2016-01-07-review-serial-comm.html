RS-232 Signals</h3>

Most Significant Bit First</i> and Least Significant Bit First</i> are indications on the ordering of the sequence of the bits in the bytes sent over a wire in a transmission protocol or in a stream (e.g. an audio stream).

Most Significant Bit First</i> means that the most significant bit will arrive first: hence e.g. the hexadecimal number0x12</code>, 00010010</code> in binary representation, will arrive as the sequence 0 0 0 1 0 0 1 0</code> .

Least Significant Bit First</i> means that the least significant bit will arrive first: hence e.g. the same hexadecimal number 0x12</code>, again 00010010</code> in binary representation, will arrive as the (reversed) sequence 0 1 0 0 1 0 0 0</code>.
